WHY SHOULD DONATE BLOOD?

Our nation requires 4 crore units of blood while 40 lakh units are available.Every two seconds, someone needs blood.There is no subtitute for human blood. It cannot be manufactured .Blood donation is an extremely noble deed,yet there is a scarcity of regular donors across india.We focus on creating and expanding a virtual army of blood donatingvolunteers who could be searched and contacted by family/care givers of a patient in times of need.

RHESUS FACTOR

The Rh factor is one of the proteins on RBCs used to indicate whether the blood of two different people is compatible when mixed – such as blood of a mother and her baby at birth.

BLOOD GROUPS

The eight major blood types are O positive,O negative,A positive,A negative,B positive,B negative,AB positive andAB negative.They are classified based on the presence of surface antigen

BOMBAY BLOOD GROUP

The Bombay blood group is a rare blood group, phenotypes of this group lacking H antigen on the red cell membrane and have anti-H in the serum. It fails to express any A, B or H antigen on their red cells or other tissues. ... People having Bombay phenotype are mostly confined to the Southeast Asia.

DONATION

Roughly 1 pint is given during a donation. A healthy donor may donate red blood cells every 56 days, or double red cells every 112 days. A healthy donor may donate platelets as few as 7 days apart, but a maximum of 24 times a year.
